Welcome to the first edition of the Deccan Ruby Conference.

19 July 2014, Pune

A one-day, single-track conference that starts early and ends late!

The Vision

Have fun while attending the Ruby conference - the tag line is “होऊ दे!” (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hou_De_Kharcha).
The conference boasts of workshops and talks for both beginners and experts.

Fun

A lavish party at a local brewery and a potential Futsal tournament among conference attendees! (After all the World Cup finals would have been just a week earlier). For people staying on till Sunday, we plan to organise a trip to Sinhagad (a fort near Pune that has some pristine scenery and a rich history that will leave you speechless). A good easy 1 hour trek for those who want more than just some football.

Education

Pune being a student city (yes - there are about 150 colleges just inside the City!), in a very unique way, we are allowing Companies to sponsor students while getting access to hiring budding Rubyists. Read more about this in the Sponsorship Prospectus!

Speakers

  • Koichi Sasada

    Ruby Core, Heroku

    Koichi Sasada is a programmer, mainly developing Ruby interpreter (CRuby/MRI). He is a member of Matz's team in Heroku, Inc. He is also a director of Ruby Association.

  • Satish Talim

    Founder RubyLearning.org

    Satish is Ruby and Go Hobbyist, Director JoshSoftware and Maybole Technologies, Author, Trainer.

  • Baishampayan Ghose

    Co-Founder & CTO at Helpshift

    Baishampayan "BG" Ghose is CTO/Co-founder at Helpshift, Inc. BG is a career functional programmer with exposure to a wide variety of programming languages and paradigms. His areas of interests are Semantics of Programming Languages, Distributed Systems, Software Design and the intersection of Software, Culture & Society.

  • Swanand Pagnis

    Hacker at Simplero

    Swanand is an active member of the Bangalore and Mysore Ruby Communities and has contributed to the awesome by bringing in a flavour of Pune, where he was born and raised. He loves building communities and discussing the craft of building software with fellow hackers as much as building software. For a living, he has been hacking code since the 8 years or so, more recently at Simplero. Ruby is his go-to language, but Clojure comes a close second.

  • Anuj Deshpande

    Embedded Systems Engineer at Revealing Hour Creations

    I build stuff. I like Open Hardware, good food and cats on the internet.

  • Rishi Jain

    Developer at Josh Software Pvt. Ltd

    Rishi started working on Ruby on Rails straight out of college at Josh Software. He has been doing web development since then and absolutely loves doing it. He has also spoken at RubyConf India, 2014 Asia edition along with various talks given at local meet-ups. When not working, he loves playing soccer, travelling and spending time with his family.

  • Sachin Shintre

    Director at Josh Software

    14+ years in Software industry, having worked on Mobile platforms, hard-core Windows systems apps, a variety of other technologies and products like MS Exchange and RIM Enterprise server. Also instrumental in conceptualizing and releasing a network security product.

  • Arnab Deka

    Journeyman software developer

    Arnab is a journey-man software developer and has been writing Ruby (i.e. having fun) since 2008. He loves to travel around the world - and has so far experienced living and working in Pune, Bangalore, NYC and Seattle (having been part of the AWS team at Amazon.com and later on at LivingSocial). Arnab has been crafting software professionally for 9 years and is more passionate about it every new day! He is a bit of a language-nut loves to experiment with different programming paradigms all the time.

  • Siddharth Sharma

    Futurist in Residence at embibe.com

    I'm probably the only IIM Bangalore grad who still makes a living deploying code to production. In previous avatars I used to be a banker, unemployed, musician, unemployed musician and microfinancier. Then I found Ruby in 2008 and never felt the need to find something else to do after that. Hou de!

  • Prashant Tiwar

When not sitting glued to a computer, he likes to jam with his musical friends, take pictures, and dig deep into philosophy and marvel at the wonders of this world.
